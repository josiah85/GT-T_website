Question: How do I obtain pre-paid BlackBerry® service?
Answer: Untitled document

To subscribe to pre-paid BlackBerry® service you must first ensure that your mobile account has at least a minimum balance of $100 plus sufficient funds to cover the BlackBerry® service fee for your preferred period of service.  If your balance is insufficient, you will receive a text message stating <Your balance can't support this charge please recharge your account and try again>.

  • Send short message stating service type (i.e. BB) and service duration (i.e. 1W = 1 week); 5D = 5 days; 2M = two months; etc.) to 6204227.  For example, text BB1W to sign up for 1 week of service.
  • The following registration confirmation message will be received: <Your GT&T BlackBerry® service request has been received>
  • Within 10 minutes re-send the same text (BB1W) to the same number (6204227) to accept the Terms of Service and activate your service.  (Note that activation will be done on the second text if it is different from the first).
  • A second message will be received: <Your confirmation message was received and your request is being processed>
  • When we have completed your service activation you will receive the following message: <Your GT&T BlackBerry® service is now active>
  • Restart your handheld device if you are a new BlackBerry® service user.
  • Your service is now registered, activated and ready for use
Question: Can I extend or reduce my period of service?
Answer: Untitled document

Yes.  Anytime after the 10-minute period of the first activation, you can send a second subscription message to extend or reduce your service period.  For example, if you had a 1-week service, sending BB2W will extend your service from 1 to 2 weeks.  You will receive a confirmation messages stating that <Your GT&T BlackBerry service period has been extended>.  Again, if your current balance is insufficient for an increased period of service you will receive a message stating that <Your balance cannot support this service extension, recharge your account and try again>
Question: What about Account Expiry and Re-subscription
Answer: Untitled document

When your subscription period has expired (or your data allocation has been exhausted) your BlackBerry® account is suspended.  You will be so notified by a message stating that <Your GT&T BlackBerry® service has been suspended because you have exceeded your service time or data allocation>.  You must re-subscribes (similar to extending your service period) to continue to enjoy the service.  Again, you will receive a message stating that <Your GT&T BlackBerry® service resumption request has been received and is being processed>.  And, after the service is reactivated you will receive a message stating that <Your GT&T BlackBerry service is now resumed>.  Note that if a suspended account is not reactivated within 45 days, the account is cancelled and you will receive the following message: <Your GT&T BlackBerry® service has been inactive for 45 days. Your service subscription has been cancelled>.  You must now re-subscribe to the service.
Question: How do I query the status of my account?
Answer: Untitled document

Text BBstatus to 6204227 to obtain the status of your account.   You will receive a message.  For example: <Your GT&T service status is active. Expiration is 2011-11-16 15:32. Your service type is daily. Please use *121# for your data balance>.  Balance queries will be followed by a message that gives your data balance.  For example, <Your current data balance is XXkb>.
Question: What happens when my pre-paid BlackBerry® service balance expires?
Answer: Untitled document

GT&T will use its best efforts to notify customers when their balance is about to be exhausted.  However, when and if your pre-paid BlackBerry® balance finishes, you will still be able to send and receive calls and text messages (provided you have credit).  Your BlackBerry® service will be reactivated immediately upon your payment of the plan fee.
Question: I have GT&T’s post-paid mobile service. Do I pay an additional fee if I take the BlackBerry® service?
Answer: Untitled document

Yes.  For post-paid customers, that fee will depend on the BlackBerry® Plan selected.  There is a monthly fee associated with each plan and customers must pay for any additional features requested. As with all post-paid services, you will receive a monthly bill which details the outstanding payment for the month completed.  The bill is due on or before the second Saturday of every month.

Question: How do I Page Down/Up in a message?
Answer: Untitled document

To Page Down: Press Space Bar to page down in an open message.

To Page Up: Press ALT+ENTER (or ALT+Space Bar) to page up in an open message.
Question: How do I quickly get to my newest message and my oldest messages?
Answer: Untitled document

Go to your Messages then press the letter "T" and it will take you to the Top of the list. Pressing "B" will take you to the bottom. Pressing the Space Bar will move you down one screen of messages at a time.
Question: How can I tell how good my phone signal is?
Answer: Untitled document

The bars on the top right of the phone indicate how strong your phone signal is.  The more bars, the better the signal.  If you want numbers instead of the bars: You can change the display of the 'signal strength' to read in real numbers.  From the home screen hold down the ALT key while you type NMLL. The bars should change to read numbers.  For the 'signal strength', you may consider anything at -90 to -50 dBm excellent.
Question: How do I password protect my BlackBerry®?
Answer: Untitled document

To set a password, go to the Options menu and select the Security item. Set the desired timeout (maybe 15 minutes) and enable/set a password.  In conjunction with this, you should also go to the Options menu to the Owner item, and enter your name, phone number, etc. (When the password protection kicks in, this is what is displayed on the screen). After five incorrect attempts at typing in your password, the password starts echoing (figuring you really need all the help you can get). After ten attempts, it performs a lobotomy on itself and almost all data is cleared.  The Memo database is retained and visible.
Question: What is BlackBerry® Messenger?
Answer: Untitled document

BlackBerry® Messenger is a RIM software that transforms PIN-to-PIN messaging into an easy Instant Messaging interface like MSN.
Question: What is a Master Reset?
Answer: Untitled document

If your BlackBerry® handheld locks up try a master Reset. Take the back door off, remove the battery, count to 10 and put the battery back.  The unit will power off then back on.  No information will be erased.
Question: What is a Master Clear?
Answer: Untitled document

If you wish to erase all of the information from your BlackBerry® device (for instance, when the handheld was used by one employee and is to be given to another), select Options, then select Security. From MENU select "WIPE HANDHELD". It will notify you that all info will be erased. The unit will power off and then back on and all the information will be erased (Email, contacts, calendar, etc).
Question: How do I get the "Help Me! " Screen?
Answer: Untitled document

Hold down the ALT and SHIFT keys AND then press the letter 'H'. This brings up the "Help Me!" screen that lists version, app version, pin, imei, uptime, signal strength, battery level, file free, and file total.
Question: How do I perform a software upgrade to my handheld from my BlackBerry® smart phone?
Answer: Untitled document

You can update your device software from your BlackBerry® smart phone by:

  • Selecting Options on your home screen.
  • Clicking Advanced Options.
  • Selecting Wireless Upgrade and following the instructions.

Note that depending on your BlackBerry® service plan, data charges may apply when downloading the update over the wireless network.
Question: How do I perform application downloads?
Answer: Untitled document

To perform application downloads,

  • Click the Browser Icon on your home screen.
  • Scroll down and click on “Visit BlackBerry® App World”.
  • Follow on-screen instructions to download & install the BlackBerry® App World Application.
  • After downloading the App World Application, the icon becomes available in the Downloads Folder under Options.

The App World Application lists all the applications which are available for your particular BlackBerry® model.  

Note, however, that Face Book application is only available to BlackBerry® Software Version’s (OS) 5, 6 & 7 and You Tube application is only available to BlackBerry® Software Version’s (OS) 6 & 7.
