Mobile - Roaming Checklist

Before you travel abroad, you should try to do everything in your power to make your roaming experience a pleasant one. Here's a checklist that will help.

  • I have checked GT&T's website to ascertain whether roaming is availability where I am headed and transiting.
  • I have familiarized myself with the use of GT&T's Roaming service.
  • I have checked on the availability of roaming networks in the country to which I am headed
  • I have verified the compatibility of my handset with the network frequency in my destination country.
  • I have inquired as to the services which are supported by the networks I may be using while abroad.
  • I am confident that I know how to make calls while roaming.
  • I am confident that I understand the roaming charging method.
  • As a pre-paid service user, I have topped up my account sufficiently.
  • I must remember to walk with my handset charger and an adaptor, in the event that the electrical outlets at my destination differ from those in Guyana.

Here's to a happy and trouble free roaming experience!

FAQ

How can I sign up for the new discounted rate to the US & Canada?

  • There is no need to sign up. This is offered to everyone in order to pass savings onto you, our customers.

What does a subscriber need to do before traveling to the US or Canada?

  • Postpaid Customers
    - Contact Customer Care to activate ‘Roaming’ on your account.
    - Review your usage limit with Customer Care to ensure it is sufficient for your anticipated trip(s).
    - If you would like to increase your usage limit, you will need to submit an additional deposit.
  • Prepaid Subscribers
    - Your account is automatically deducted as you place/receive calls so there is no need to do anything prior to traveling

What does a customer need to do when they arrive in the US or Canada?

  • Power on your mobile
  • Select Settings
  • Select Network
  • Select Manual Network
  • Select Roaming Partner (AT&T in the US; Bell or Telus in Canada; Rogers in Canada if only option)
    - Note – Rogers currently does not have data available for our customers

What does the new discounted rate include?

GYD Figures

  • $72/minute for both inbound and outbound calls while roaming in Canada and the United States. (includes calls made to US, to Canada and back to Guyana)
  • $31/SMS Mobile Origination (MO) when in the US or Canada
  • No Charge for SMS Mobile Termination (MT)
  • $0.10/KB for data sessions while roaming in Canada and the United States
  • No Subscription Fee (unique to GT&T, competitors charge a fee)

USD Figures

  • $0.35/minute for both inbound and outbound calls while roaming in Canada and the United States. (includes calls made to US, to Canada and back to Guyana)
  • $0.15/SMS MO when in the US or Canada
  • No Charge for SMS MT
  • $0.0005/KB for data sessions while roaming in Canada and the United States

Am I charged the new discounted rate everywhere I roam?

  • You will receive the special discounted rate (with NO monthly subscription fee) when you roam anywhere in Canada or in AT&T Wireless coverage areas in the United States
  • Only currently able to roam on AT&T in the US, so there will not be any surprise roaming charges from a different US Carrier

GT&T Customer roaming in United States and unable to make/place call

  • Check to ensure customer is in AT&T coverage area
  • Only AT&T service is currently available to GT&T customers

Why are the Caribbean Islands not included in the new discounted rate?

  • We are working to reduce costs for our customers in the Caribbean islands as well – as soon as this is possible, we will roll out to the customers

What frequency bands are in Canada & US

  • 850 and 1900

How do I dial a local call or call back to Guyana while roaming?

  • For a local call in the United States or Canada, Customers should dial the area code and number.
  • For a call to Guyana from the United States or Canada, Customers must use the + key and the number they are trying to reach (which needs to include the country code of 592)
  • For calls to other countries while roaming in the United States or Canada, Customers must use the + key and the number they are trying to reach (including the country code).
